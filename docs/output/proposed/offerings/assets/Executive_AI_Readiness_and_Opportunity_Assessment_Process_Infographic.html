Process Overview

Executive AI Readiness and Opportunity Assessment

A stage-based assessment process designed to help leadership understand current AI readiness, identify the strongest opportunity areas, surface the main risks, and decide the next move worth funding.

Assessment Purpose

The process combines readiness review, opportunity prioritization, and risk analysis so leadership can make a practical decision with clarity.

Core Stages

The Assessment Follows A Common Six-Stage Process

The process moves from sponsor alignment and current-state review into readiness analysis, opportunity prioritization, risk synthesis, and a practical executive recommendation.

1. Sponsor Alignment

Confirm the executive decision context, timing, scope, and what leadership needs to decide at the end.

2. Current-State Evidence Review

Gather the available information on AI activity, priorities, capability signals, and existing constraints.

3. Readiness Assessment

Assess strategy, governance, data, platform, ownership, talent, and change conditions.

4. Opportunity Review

Identify the most credible opportunity areas based on value, feasibility, and timing.

5. Risk And Constraint View

Surface the main blockers, exposures, and execution risks shaping what should happen next.

6. Recommendation And Readout

Translate the findings into a clear recommendation and 90-day action path for leadership.

Assessment Variants

Two Delivery Paths Support Different Decision Needs

Quickfire Assessment

Fast Executive Clarity

  • 2-week assessment window
  • focused leadership and stakeholder interviews
  • targeted evidence review
  • emphasis on readiness, opportunities, risks, and the immediate next move
Detailed Assessment

Deeper Prioritization View

  • 4 to 6 week assessment window
  • broader stakeholder participation
  • deeper capability and portfolio analysis
  • greater emphasis on dependencies, sequencing, and follow-on execution

Shared Decision Outcomes

  • launch focused pilot
  • run opportunity prioritization sprint
  • strengthen foundations first
  • pause and reframe

Quickfire Timeline

  • Week 1: sponsor framing, interviews, evidence review
  • Week 2: synthesis, recommendation development, executive readout

Detailed Timeline

  • Weeks 1-2: scope, stakeholder mapping, evidence collection
  • Weeks 3-4: readiness, opportunity, and risk analysis
  • Weeks 5-6 if required: validation and final readout
