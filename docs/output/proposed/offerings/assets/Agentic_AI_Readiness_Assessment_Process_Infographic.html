Process Overview

Enterprise Agentic AI Readiness Assessment

A stage-based assessment process designed to help leadership understand where agentic AI is credible, what is ready for pilot now, and what conditions need to be addressed before moving forward.

Assessment Purpose

The process combines workflow suitability review, readiness analysis, and recommendation development so leadership can make practical decisions with confidence.

Core Stages

The Assessment Follows A Common Six-Stage Process

The process moves from scope definition and evidence gathering into suitability review, readiness analysis, recommendation development, and executive decision support.

1. Scope And Alignment

Confirm the sponsor objective, workflows in scope, key stakeholders, evidence sources, and the decision that needs to be supported.

2. Evidence Collection

Gather the workflow materials, stakeholder input, and current-state context needed to build a grounded view.

3. Workflow Suitability Review

Assess whether agentic AI is a strong fit for each workflow based on how the work actually operates.

4. Readiness And Controls Review

Review governance, oversight, observability, accountability, and control conditions needed for safe execution.

5. Recommendation Development

Translate findings into recommendations, supporting rationale, conditions, and next steps for each workflow.

6. Executive Readout

Present a concise view of the findings, recommendation set, and immediate path forward.

Assessment Variants

Two Delivery Paths Support Different Decision Needs

Quickfire Assessment

Fast Decision Support

  • 2-week assessment window
  • typically 2 to 4 workflows
  • focused stakeholder interviews and evidence review
  • emphasis on fit, readiness, control gaps, and immediate next move
  • well suited to a near-term pilot decision
Detailed Assessment

Deeper Pilot-Readiness View

  • 4 to 6 week assessment window
  • typically 3 to 6 workflows, depending on complexity
  • broader stakeholder participation and deeper current-state review
  • greater focus on dependencies, controls, alignment, and pilot planning
  • well suited to larger or more complex workflow decisions

Quickfire Timeline

  • Week 1: scope, interviews, evidence review, early fit assessment
  • Week 2: readiness review, recommendations, executive readout

Detailed Timeline

  • Weeks 1-2: scope, stakeholder mapping, evidence collection
  • Weeks 3-4: suitability and readiness analysis
  • Weeks 5-6 if required: alignment, refinement, and final readout

Shared Decision Outcomes

  • proceed to pilot
  • proceed with narrowed scope
  • defer until gaps are closed
  • do not pursue

Deliverables

The Output Set Is Designed For Decision Clarity And Practical Next Steps

The final output combines executive synthesis, workflow-level assessment, and a practical next-step path so the organization can act with clarity.

Executive Decision Summary

  • decision context and scope
  • overall recommendation view
  • top risks and conditions
  • immediate next-step direction

Workflow Assessment Pack

  • workflow suitability scorecards
  • readiness heatmap
  • governance and control gap view
  • recommendation sheet by workflow

Next-Step Path

  • 90-day next-step plan
  • pilot-readiness considerations
  • ownership and decision gates
  • follow-on support where required

Closing View

The purpose of the assessment is to provide a practical, well-supported view of what should move forward, under what conditions, and at what pace.
