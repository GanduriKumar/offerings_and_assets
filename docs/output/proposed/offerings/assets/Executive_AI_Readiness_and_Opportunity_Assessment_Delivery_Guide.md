# Executive AI Readiness and Opportunity Assessment Delivery Guide

## Purpose
This guide explains how the Executive AI Readiness and Opportunity Assessment is delivered in two versions:

- `Quickfire Assessment`: a 2-week assessment designed to give leadership a fast, credible outside view
- `Detailed Assessment`: a 4 to 6 week assessment designed for a deeper readiness, opportunity, and next-step review

In both versions, the objective is the same: help leadership understand how ready the organization is for broader AI action, where the strongest opportunity areas sit, what the main risks are, and what should happen next.

## What The Assessment Is Designed To Do
The assessment is intended to help leadership answer four practical questions:

1. How ready is the organization to move beyond isolated AI activity?
2. Where are the best practical opportunity areas right now?
3. What risks, gaps, or constraints matter most before scaling effort?
4. What should leadership do next: proceed, narrow focus, strengthen foundations, or wait?

## Assessment Variants

### Quickfire Assessment
Best used when leadership wants clarity before committing budget or expanding activity.

- duration: 2 weeks
- scope: leadership interviews, current-state review, opportunity scan, and synthesis
- focus: readiness picture, opportunity themes, risk view, and immediate next move
- output emphasis: executive decision support

### Detailed Assessment
Best used when the organization wants stronger support for sequencing, prioritization, and next-step design.

- duration: 4 to 6 weeks
- scope: wider stakeholder review, deeper capability analysis, opportunity prioritization, and execution implications
- focus: readiness, opportunity, risks, dependencies, and 90-day path
- output emphasis: decision support plus sharper follow-on planning

## Core Process Stages
The assessment follows the same broad process in both variants. The difference is the depth of evidence, number of stakeholders, and level of detail in the output.

### Stage 1. Sponsor Alignment And Decision Framing
Objective: confirm why the assessment is being run and what executive decision it needs to support.

Typical activities:
- confirm leadership objectives and urgency
- understand current AI activity and investment context
- define the final readout audience and decision needs
- confirm assessment scope and timing

Typical outputs:
- assessment scope note
- sponsor objective summary
- interview map

### Stage 2. Current-State Evidence Review
Objective: understand the organization’s current AI position, known initiatives, capabilities, and constraints.

Typical activities:
- review current AI initiatives and ambitions
- examine existing plans, pilots, and capability signals
- gather views on governance, data, platform, and adoption conditions
- identify gaps in evidence early

Typical outputs:
- current-state notes
- evidence log
- early readiness observations

### Stage 3. Readiness Assessment
Objective: assess whether the organization has the conditions required to make disciplined progress with AI.

Typical review dimensions:
- strategy and executive sponsorship
- use-case prioritization discipline
- data and platform readiness
- governance and risk management
- operating-model ownership
- talent, capability, and change readiness

Typical outputs:
- readiness heatmap
- readiness summary

### Stage 4. Opportunity Review
Objective: identify and prioritize the most credible opportunity areas.

Typical review dimensions:
- business relevance
- feasibility
- value potential
- timing
- risk level
- dependency on stronger foundations

Typical outputs:
- opportunity shortlist
- opportunity rationale

### Stage 5. Risk And Constraint View
Objective: make the major blockers, exposures, and execution risks visible before leadership commits more effort.

Typical review dimensions:
- governance gaps
- data and technology limitations
- security, privacy, and compliance concerns
- ownership ambiguity
- delivery capacity and change risk

Typical outputs:
- risk and constraint summary
- conditions-to-proceed view

### Stage 6. Recommendation Development And Executive Readout
Objective: turn the findings into a clear recommendation and a practical next-step path.

Typical outputs:
- executive summary
- recommended next action
- 90-day action path
- readout deck

## Quickfire Assessment Delivery

### When To Use It
Use the Quickfire Assessment when leadership wants a credible outside view quickly before committing wider effort or budget.

### Typical Shape
- senior-led assessment
- focused interview set
- targeted review of current AI activity and plans
- concise readiness and opportunity synthesis

### Typical Timeline

#### Week 1
- kickoff and sponsor alignment
- leadership interviews
- current-state evidence review
- early readiness and opportunity hypotheses

#### Week 2
- readiness and risk synthesis
- opportunity prioritization
- next-step recommendation development
- executive readout

### Quickfire Deliverables
- executive AI readiness heatmap
- opportunity shortlist
- risk and constraint view
- recommended next action
- 90-day action path

### What Makes The Quickfire Useful
- provides clarity quickly
- helps leadership reduce confusion before scaling effort
- exposes obvious gaps without overcomplicating the engagement
- supports a practical funding or sequencing decision

## Detailed Assessment Delivery

### When To Use It
Use the Detailed Assessment when the organization wants a stronger basis for prioritization, sequencing, and follow-on action.

### Typical Shape
- wider stakeholder participation
- deeper review of current AI activity and capability conditions
- more structured opportunity assessment
- stronger emphasis on dependencies, risks, and execution implications

### Typical Timeline

#### Week 1
- sponsor alignment
- scope definition
- stakeholder and evidence mapping

#### Week 2
- leadership, business, and functional interviews
- current-state review
- early readiness view

#### Week 3
- deeper capability and risk analysis
- opportunity area testing

#### Week 4
- prioritization and recommendation development
- early 90-day path shaping

#### Weeks 5 To 6, If Required
- additional validation
- refinement of output set
- final executive readout

### Detailed Deliverables
- executive AI readiness heatmap
- opportunity shortlist with rationale
- risk and constraint register
- recommendation summary
- 90-day action path
- optional workshop or follow-on discussion pack

### What Makes The Detailed Assessment Useful
- creates a stronger basis for executive prioritization
- shows where the organization is genuinely ready versus overreaching
- connects opportunity selection with practical constraints
- supports a cleaner bridge into pilots, governance work, or roadmap activity

## Deliverable Descriptions

### Executive AI Readiness Heatmap
A concise view of current capability strength across the areas that matter most for disciplined AI progress.

### Opportunity Shortlist
A prioritized set of the most credible opportunity areas, including why they matter and what conditions affect them.

### Risk And Constraint View
A focused summary of the main risks, blockers, and capability gaps that should shape decisions.

### Recommended Next Action
A direct recommendation on the next move leadership should take.

### 90-Day Action Path
A structured view of what should happen next if leadership chooses to move forward.

## Client Participation
The assessment depends on timely access to the right leaders, documents, and current-state context.

Typical client participation includes:
- executive sponsor alignment
- business and functional stakeholder interviews
- sharing of current AI plans, pilots, or relevant current-state materials
- access to governance, technology, and operating-model context

## How The Two Variants Differ

| Dimension | Quickfire Assessment | Detailed Assessment |
|---|---|---|
| Duration | 2 weeks | 4 to 6 weeks |
| Stakeholder depth | focused | broader |
| Evidence depth | targeted | deeper |
| Output emphasis | immediate executive clarity | decision support plus sharper prioritization |
| Best use case | before committing budget or wider effort | before sequencing broader follow-on action |

## Closing Note
Both variants are designed to create practical executive clarity, not abstract commentary. The value of the process lies in showing leadership where they really stand, what opportunities are worth serious attention, and what the next move should be.
