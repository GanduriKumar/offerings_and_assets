# Enterprise Agentic AI Readiness Assessment Client Preparation Checklists

## Purpose
This document helps the client prepare for the Enterprise Agentic AI Readiness Assessment before the assessment begins.

It is designed to reduce delays, improve the quality of evidence available during the review, and ensure the assessment can focus on the right workflows, stakeholders, and decisions from the start.

Two preparation tracks are provided:

- `Quickfire Assessment`: for the 2-week assessment
- `Detailed Assessment`: for the 4 to 6 week assessment

## How To Use This Document
- use the relevant checklist based on the assessment variant agreed
- identify an internal coordinator to track completion
- gather only the materials that already exist; there is no need to create perfect documentation from scratch
- if an item is unavailable, note that clearly rather than leaving it ambiguous
- where possible, provide real workflow examples rather than only summary descriptions

## Section A. Quickfire Assessment Client Preparation Checklist

### 1. Executive Sponsor And Decision Context
- Confirm the executive sponsor for the assessment.
- Confirm the primary business decision the assessment needs to support.
- Confirm why the organization is assessing agentic AI at this point.
- Confirm whether the immediate goal is pilot selection, scope narrowing, risk review, or a go / no-go decision.
- Confirm the expected date for the final executive readout.
- Confirm who will attend the final readout and who will make or influence the next decision.

### 2. Workflow Selection
- Identify 2 to 4 workflows for assessment.
- Provide a short name and one-line description for each workflow.
- Confirm why each workflow has been shortlisted.
- Confirm which business function owns each workflow.
- Confirm which operational team runs each workflow today.
- Confirm whether any of the workflows are already under redesign or automation review.
- Identify whether any shortlisted workflows are especially sensitive because of customer impact, compliance, regulated decisions, or financial exposure.

### 3. Workflow Summaries
For each workflow in scope, prepare a basic summary covering:
- workflow objective
- trigger or starting point
- main steps in the process
- systems or tools used
- human roles involved
- key outputs or outcomes
- common exceptions or escalation points
- known pain points today

### 4. Stakeholder Availability
- Identify the business owner for each workflow.
- Identify the delivery, operations, or process owner for each workflow.
- Identify the technology or platform contact where relevant.
- Confirm who can explain current approval points and control steps.
- Confirm who can explain exception handling and escalation paths.
- Ensure these stakeholders are available for focused interviews during the assessment window.
- Share preferred meeting windows in advance to avoid scheduling delays.

### 5. Current-State Materials
Gather any of the following that already exist:
- process maps
- SOPs or work instructions
- workflow screenshots
- training guides
- policy or approval notes
- sample intake forms
- sample outputs or reports
- workflow tracking sheets

If no formal documents exist, prepare a simple written summary instead.

### 6. Systems And Tooling Context
For each workflow, prepare a simple list of:
- systems used
- tools used
- key data sources
- access dependencies
- approval systems if any
- handoffs between systems or teams

Also note:
- whether the workflow depends on manual copy-paste across systems
- whether the workflow requires multiple tools to complete
- whether the workflow relies on unofficial workarounds

### 7. Performance And Outcome Signals
Where available, share current measures such as:
- cycle time
- backlog
- SLA performance
- error rates
- quality metrics
- customer impact measures
- productivity signals
- rework volumes

If hard metrics are not available, provide directional indications such as:
- high volume
- slow turnaround
- frequent escalation
- inconsistent quality
- heavy manual effort

### 8. Risk, Control, And Governance Inputs
For each workflow, note any known requirements or constraints relating to:
- approvals
- auditability
- customer communications
- sensitive data
- permissions and access
- compliance obligations
- legal or policy restrictions
- human review requirements

Also identify:
- who is accountable if the workflow fails
- what happens today when the workflow goes wrong
- whether there is an escalation path

### 9. Sample Cases
Prepare 2 to 5 representative examples per workflow where possible:
- typical case
- slightly complex case
- exception case if available

For each example, provide:
- what triggered the work
- what happened
- what systems or people were involved
- what result was produced

### 10. Internal Readiness For The Assessment
- Nominate one assessment coordinator from the client side.
- Confirm where materials will be shared.
- Confirm who will answer follow-up clarification questions during the two-week window.
- Confirm whether any materials need redaction before sharing.
- Confirm any access restrictions that may affect the evidence review.

### Quickfire Completion Check
Before the assessment starts, confirm:
- the sponsor is aligned
- the workflow list is finalized
- interviews can be scheduled
- current-state materials are available
- sample cases are ready where possible
- known control issues are disclosed early

## Section B. Detailed Assessment Client Preparation Checklist

### 1. Executive Sponsorship And Assessment Intent
- Confirm the executive sponsor and any additional sponsors or steering stakeholders.
- Confirm the primary decision the Detailed Assessment is intended to support.
- Confirm whether the broader intent is pilot planning, workflow portfolio prioritization, governance shaping, or operating-model preparation.
- Confirm the desired outcomes from the final readout.
- Confirm who needs to be aligned before the organization can act on the recommendations.
- Confirm the overall assessment window and any key decision deadlines that sit around it.

### 2. Workflow Portfolio Definition
- Identify 3 to 6 workflows for detailed assessment.
- Provide a short summary for each workflow.
- Explain why each workflow matters to the organization.
- Clarify whether each workflow is customer-facing, internal-facing, operational, analytical, or decision-support oriented.
- Confirm whether the workflows are independent or linked across a broader process chain.
- Identify which workflows are highest priority and why.
- Identify whether any workflows are already being considered for AI, automation, or process redesign.

### 3. Deeper Workflow Documentation
For each workflow, prepare available information covering:
- current process description
- process map or swimlane if available
- intake triggers
- end-to-end steps
- handoffs between teams
- approvals and review points
- business rules
- exception paths
- escalation logic
- final outputs
- rework patterns

If formal process documentation does not exist, arrange for a knowledgeable stakeholder to walk through the process in detail during the assessment.

### 4. Stakeholder Map
Prepare a fuller stakeholder list covering:
- executive sponsor
- business owner
- process owner
- operations lead
- delivery lead
- technology or platform lead
- data owner where relevant
- risk, compliance, or governance contact where relevant
- support or service owner where relevant

For each stakeholder, note:
- role
- relationship to the workflow
- expected contribution to the assessment
- availability during the assessment period

### 5. Governance And Control Context
Prepare any available material related to:
- policy requirements
- approval requirements
- data handling rules
- audit or traceability requirements
- human review expectations
- incident or failure response procedures
- security access policies
- role-based permissions
- regulatory considerations where applicable

Also be ready to explain:
- how decisions are currently approved
- how exceptions are currently handled
- how errors are currently detected
- who takes ownership when things go wrong

### 6. Systems, Data, And Tooling Landscape
For each workflow, provide:
- systems used
- tools used
- source data involved
- external data dependencies if any
- integrations used today
- manual transfers between systems
- reporting tools involved
- access boundaries

Also note:
- whether the workflow depends on structured or unstructured information
- whether there are known data-quality issues
- whether context is fragmented across multiple repositories
- whether staff rely on email, chat, spreadsheets, or undocumented workarounds to complete the work

### 7. Performance, Quality, And Operational Measures
Where available, gather:
- workflow volume
- seasonality or peaks
- cycle time
- service-level measures
- backlog
- throughput
- quality or defect metrics
- rework frequency
- customer satisfaction indicators
- compliance incidents or near misses

If exact data is not available, provide directional context and examples.

### 8. Representative Cases And Variants
Prepare a wider range of examples than in the Quickfire track.

Where possible, include:
- standard case
- high-volume case
- low-volume but high-risk case
- exception case
- escalated case
- case involving multiple teams or systems

For each example, capture:
- intake context
- steps taken
- tools and systems used
- people involved
- approval points
- result produced
- issues encountered

### 9. Existing Change Activity
Identify any active or recent initiatives related to:
- process improvement
- workflow redesign
- automation
- AI experimentation
- service transformation
- governance strengthening
- platform modernization

This helps the assessment account for overlapping work and avoid making disconnected recommendations.

### 10. Pilot And Change Readiness Context
Prepare a view of the organization’s current ability to support a pilot if one is recommended.

This should include:
- likely business owner for a pilot
- likely operational owner for a pilot
- likely technology owner for a pilot
- support model expectations
- oversight expectations
- review cadence expectations
- decision forums that would approve next steps

### 11. Internal Coordination For A Longer Assessment Window
- Nominate a client coordinator for the assessment.
- Confirm where files and notes will be shared.
- Confirm how stakeholder access will be managed across multiple weeks.
- Confirm how follow-up questions should be routed.
- Confirm what must be redacted before sharing.
- Confirm any confidentiality or access constraints that may affect analysis.

### 12. Optional But Helpful Inputs
If available, the following can improve the quality of the Detailed Assessment:
- internal strategy notes relevant to workflow transformation
- existing risk assessments
- internal audit observations
- prior pilot lessons learned
- post-incident reviews
- team capacity assumptions
- existing operating-model documents

### Detailed Assessment Completion Check
Before the assessment starts, confirm:
- the workflow portfolio is defined
- key stakeholders are identified and available
- current-state materials are assembled
- representative examples are available
- known controls and risks are understood at a basic level
- overlapping transformation activity is disclosed
- internal coordination for the assessment period is in place

## Recommended Client Submission Format
To keep collection practical, the client can submit materials in any of the following ways:
- existing documents in a shared folder
- a simple spreadsheet listing workflows, owners, and artifacts
- a written summary document per workflow
- a mix of documents and interview walkthroughs

Perfect documentation is not required. What matters most is that the assessment team gets a clear, accurate view of how the workflows operate today and what constraints shape them.

## Suggested Cover Message To Share With The Client
You can share the checklist with the following note:

```text
To help the assessment start efficiently, please use the relevant preparation checklist to gather the available workflow, stakeholder, and control information in advance. Existing materials are sufficient; there is no need to create a polished document set from scratch. If any information is unavailable, please note that clearly so we can address it during the assessment.
```
