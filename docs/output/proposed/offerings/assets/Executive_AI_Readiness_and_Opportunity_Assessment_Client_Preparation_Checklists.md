# Executive AI Readiness and Opportunity Assessment Client Preparation Checklists

## Purpose
This document helps the client prepare for the Executive AI Readiness and Opportunity Assessment before the assessment begins.

It is designed to reduce delays, improve the quality of evidence available during the review, and ensure the assessment starts with a clear view of leadership intent, current AI activity, and the most relevant constraints.

Two preparation tracks are provided:

- `Quickfire Assessment`: for the 2-week assessment
- `Detailed Assessment`: for the 4 to 6 week assessment

## How To Use This Document
- use the relevant checklist based on the agreed assessment variant
- nominate a client coordinator to track completion
- gather existing material only; there is no need to produce a polished pack from scratch
- if information is unavailable, note that clearly
- provide specific examples where possible rather than only broad statements

## Section A. Quickfire Assessment Client Preparation Checklist

### 1. Executive Sponsor And Decision Context
- Confirm the executive sponsor for the assessment.
- Confirm the primary decision the assessment needs to support.
- Confirm why the organization wants an AI readiness and opportunity view now.
- Confirm what leadership expects to decide after the readout.
- Confirm the target date for the final readout.
- Confirm who will attend the readout and who will influence the next decision.

### 2. Current AI Activity Snapshot
- Provide a short summary of any current AI initiatives, pilots, or active discussions.
- Identify whether activity is exploratory, operational, or already budgeted.
- Note whether activity is concentrated in one function or spread across the organization.
- Identify any initiatives leadership already sees as high priority.
- Note where there is visible confusion, overlap, or duplication.

### 3. Business Priorities And Opportunity Areas
- Identify the main business priorities leadership wants AI to support.
- Note where there is pressure to improve productivity, service, speed, insight, or cost.
- Identify any functions already asking for AI support.
- Note where opportunity discussions feel most concrete.
- Note where opportunity discussions are still vague or hype-driven.

### 4. Stakeholder Availability
- Identify the executive sponsor for interviews.
- Identify the main business or functional stakeholders who should be interviewed.
- Identify the technology, data, security, risk, or governance contacts where relevant.
- Confirm who can explain current AI decision-making and ownership.
- Confirm who can explain constraints, policy expectations, or platform realities.
- Share likely meeting windows to avoid delays.

### 5. Existing Materials
Gather any of the following that already exist:
- AI strategy notes
- pilot summaries
- presentation decks on AI priorities
- use-case lists
- roadmap slides
- governance notes
- policy summaries
- operating-model notes

If formal material does not exist, provide a short written summary instead.

### 6. Data, Technology, And Platform Context
Prepare a simple view covering:
- current data platform context
- main systems relevant to AI activity
- tooling already in use
- model or vendor choices already being discussed
- integration limitations
- access and security constraints

Also note:
- whether teams rely heavily on spreadsheets, email, or manual workarounds
- whether data quality or fragmentation is already a known concern
- whether AI experiments are happening outside a common platform approach

### 7. Governance, Risk, And Control Inputs
For the current AI context, note any known requirements or concerns relating to:
- data privacy
- security
- model risk
- regulatory exposure
- legal or compliance review
- human oversight expectations
- auditability
- approval requirements

Also identify:
- who owns AI-related policy decisions today
- what happens when AI-related concerns are raised
- whether there is a known escalation path

### 8. Capability And Adoption Context
- Identify the teams most involved in current AI activity.
- Note whether AI skills across the organization are concentrated or thinly spread.
- Note whether the organization has delivery capacity for follow-on work.
- Identify any change-readiness concerns.
- Note whether there is enthusiasm without clear ownership.
- Note whether leadership alignment is strong, mixed, or weak.

### 9. Sample Evidence And Examples
Prepare 2 to 5 examples where possible showing:
- a use case already discussed or tested
- a process or team that appears promising
- a case where AI interest exists but execution barriers are visible
- a case where a proposed idea was rejected or deferred

For each example, provide:
- the business context
- what the idea or use case is
- why it matters
- what is known about feasibility or constraints

### 10. Assessment Readiness
- Nominate one assessment coordinator from the client side.
- Confirm where materials will be shared.
- Confirm who will answer follow-up questions during the assessment window.
- Confirm whether any materials require redaction before sharing.
- Confirm any confidentiality constraints that may affect evidence review.

### Quickfire Completion Check
Before the assessment starts, confirm:
- the sponsor is aligned
- the interview list is identified
- current AI activity is visible at a basic level
- relevant materials are available where possible
- major constraints are disclosed early
- the readout audience is known

## Section B. Detailed Assessment Client Preparation Checklist

### 1. Executive Sponsorship And Assessment Intent
- Confirm the executive sponsor and any other leadership stakeholders who need to be aligned.
- Confirm the main decisions this assessment is expected to support.
- Confirm whether the broader intent is prioritization, readiness clarification, governance shaping, pilot selection, or roadmap preparation.
- Confirm the desired outcomes from the final readout.
- Confirm the assessment timeline and any nearby budget or decision deadlines.

### 2. Current AI Portfolio View
- Identify current AI initiatives, pilots, experiments, and proposed ideas.
- Note which efforts are active, paused, planned, or only under discussion.
- Confirm which areas already have budget or executive attention.
- Identify where there is overlap, duplication, or inconsistent ownership.
- Identify which efforts appear strongest and weakest today.

### 3. Business Opportunity Context
- Provide a fuller view of the major business priorities where AI may create value.
- Clarify which functions or processes are under most pressure to improve.
- Note whether opportunity areas are customer-facing, workforce productivity, decision-support, analytical, or operational.
- Identify which opportunity areas leadership sees as strategically important.
- Identify where there is visible urgency versus long-term interest only.

### 4. Stakeholder Map
Prepare a fuller stakeholder list covering:
- executive sponsor
- business leaders
- digital or transformation leads
- technology leaders
- data leaders
- security or risk contacts
- legal or compliance contacts where relevant
- delivery or operations leaders where relevant

For each stakeholder, note:
- role
- relationship to AI activity
- expected contribution to the assessment
- availability during the assessment period

### 5. Governance And Decision-Making Context
Prepare any available material related to:
- AI policies
- approval processes
- risk review expectations
- legal or compliance review points
- data usage rules
- human oversight requirements
- incident handling expectations
- ownership of decision rights

Also be ready to explain:
- how AI initiatives are approved today
- who can stop or defer activity
- how exceptions are handled
- how accountability works when uncertainty is high

### 6. Data, Platform, And Tooling Landscape
Provide a fuller view of:
- major data sources relevant to AI work
- data quality issues
- platform choices or constraints
- integration limitations
- vendor dependencies
- access boundaries
- fragmented tooling
- shadow AI activity if known

Also note:
- whether context is spread across multiple systems
- whether access restrictions block practical progress
- whether there is a common path for deployment or experimentation

### 7. Performance, Value, And Operational Signals
Where available, gather:
- productivity signals
- service or turnaround issues
- quality issues
- cost pressure
- backlog or throughput concerns
- customer or stakeholder pain points
- missed opportunity themes
- measures already used to justify AI interest

If exact metrics are not available, provide directional context and examples.

### 8. Opportunity Examples And Variants
Prepare a broader range of examples than in the Quickfire track.

Where possible, include:
- obvious near-term opportunity
- longer-term strategic opportunity
- high-value but high-risk case
- feasible but lower-value case
- case already tested or discussed
- case that should probably be deprioritized

For each example, capture:
- business context
- expected value
- dependencies
- likely risks
- who would need to own it

### 9. Existing Change Activity
Identify any active or recent initiatives related to:
- AI experimentation
- data modernization
- process transformation
- platform changes
- governance strengthening
- operating-model redesign
- workforce capability building

This helps the assessment avoid producing disconnected recommendations.

### 10. Follow-On Execution Context
Prepare a view of the organization’s current ability to act on a recommendation.

This should include:
- likely owners for follow-on work
- likely delivery capacity
- expected governance forums
- likely funding route
- pilot support expectations
- change and adoption expectations

### 11. Assessment Coordination For A Longer Assessment Window
- Nominate a client coordinator for the assessment.
- Confirm where materials and notes will be shared.
- Confirm how follow-up questions should be routed.
- Confirm what must be redacted before sharing.
- Confirm any confidentiality or access restrictions.

### 12. Optional But Helpful Inputs
If available, the following can improve the Detailed Assessment:
- strategy notes
- investment or budget discussion materials
- risk or audit observations
- prior pilot retrospectives
- platform roadmaps
- capability-building plans
- operating-model documents

### Detailed Assessment Completion Check
Before the assessment starts, confirm:
- the sponsor group is aligned
- current AI activity is visible at a usable level
- key stakeholders are identified and available
- current materials are assembled
- major governance and platform constraints are disclosed
- overlapping transformation work is visible
- assessment coordination is in place

## Recommended Client Submission Format
To keep collection practical, the client can submit materials in any of the following ways:
- existing files in a shared folder
- a spreadsheet listing initiatives, owners, and artifacts
- a short written summary pack
- a mix of documents and interview walkthroughs

Perfect documentation is not required. What matters most is a clear and honest view of current AI activity, opportunity areas, constraints, and decision context.
