# Executive AI Readiness and Opportunity Assessment Finding Capture Checklists

## Purpose
This working document supports consistent capture of findings during delivery while assessing readiness, opportunities, risks, and recommended next steps.

Two finding-capture tracks are provided:

- `Quickfire Assessment`: for the 2-week executive review
- `Detailed Assessment`: for the deeper 4 to 6 week review

## How To Use This Document
- use the relevant checklist during interviews, evidence review, and synthesis
- capture facts first and interpretation second
- note missing evidence explicitly
- separate current-state observations from recommendations
- show where leadership alignment is strong, weak, or assumed

## Section A. Quickfire Assessment Finding Capture Checklist

### 1. Sponsor Intent And Decision Framing
- Confirm the sponsor objective in plain language.
- Capture the exact decision leadership wants to make.
- Note what must be decided now versus later.
- Capture any explicit scope limits.
- Note the urgency behind the assessment.
- Record what the final recommendation needs to answer.

### 2. Current AI Activity Findings
- Capture what AI work is already underway.
- Note which initiatives are exploratory, pilot-stage, or more developed.
- Capture where activity is concentrated across functions.
- Note where ownership is clear and where it is fragmented.
- Record where enthusiasm is high but evidence is thin.
- Record where activity appears duplicated or disconnected.

### 3. Readiness Findings
Capture findings on:
- executive sponsorship and leadership alignment
- business prioritization discipline
- data readiness
- platform and tooling readiness
- governance and risk management
- operating-model ownership
- talent and capability readiness
- change and adoption readiness

Record:
- areas that appear genuinely ready
- areas that are partial or inconsistent
- areas that are likely blockers to scaling effort
- overall readiness label: `Ready To Advance`, `Ready With Conditions`, `Not Ready Yet`, `Pause And Reframe`

### 4. Opportunity Findings
For each major opportunity area, capture:
- business problem being addressed
- why it matters now
- expected value type
- practical feasibility
- likely dependencies
- likely owner
- timing relevance

Record:
- strongest opportunity areas
- weaker or less credible opportunity areas
- where value is strong but readiness is weak
- where opportunity talk is generic rather than actionable

### 5. Risk And Constraint Findings
Capture findings on:
- governance gaps
- security or privacy concerns
- legal or compliance concerns
- data limitations
- platform limitations
- delivery capacity constraints
- ownership ambiguity
- change or adoption risks

Record:
- issues that could block follow-on work immediately
- issues that can be managed with conditions
- risks leadership may be underestimating

### 6. Evidence Quality
For the assessment overall, note:
- what evidence was reviewed
- whether evidence was direct or second-hand
- whether examples were representative
- whether metrics were available
- where assumptions had to be made
- where follow-up evidence is still needed

### 7. Recommended Next Action Formation
Capture:
- the strongest recommendation emerging
- the reasons supporting it
- what should be prioritized first
- what should explicitly not be prioritized yet
- which conditions must be met to move forward
- likely owners for the next move
- target timing for the next decision

### 8. Final Quickfire Readiness Check
Before finalizing the output, confirm:
- the readiness view is clear enough for executive discussion
- the opportunity shortlist is prioritized
- the risk view is explicit
- the recommendation is evidence-based
- weak or premature ideas are visibly deprioritized
- the next move is clear enough to fund or defer

## Section B. Detailed Assessment Finding Capture Checklist

### 1. Assessment Intent And Enterprise Context
- Capture the sponsor objective and broader assessment intent.
- Record the executive decisions this assessment is expected to support.
- Capture strategic context relevant to AI prioritization.
- Note broader business or transformation pressures shaping urgency.
- Record major dependencies on other change activity.

### 2. Current AI Portfolio Findings
Capture:
- active initiatives
- planned initiatives
- experimental activity
- informal or shadow activity
- initiative ownership
- overlap across efforts
- signs of portfolio sprawl
- signs of strong focus

Also note:
- which efforts appear most credible
- which efforts appear weak or unsupported

### 3. Detailed Readiness Findings
Capture findings on:
- executive sponsorship
- strategic clarity
- opportunity selection discipline
- data readiness
- platform readiness
- governance maturity
- risk management capability
- operating-model ownership
- funding and delivery capacity
- capability and change readiness

Also record:
- which gaps are fundamental blockers
- which gaps are manageable conditions
- which gaps can be handled in follow-on work
- overall readiness label: `Ready To Advance`, `Ready With Conditions`, `Not Ready Yet`, `Pause And Reframe`

### 4. Opportunity Portfolio Findings
For each opportunity area in scope, capture:
- business relevance
- expected value type
- likely scale of impact
- practical feasibility
- dependency on stronger foundations
- likely ownership
- near-term versus longer-term timing
- major counterpoints

Also note:
- where opportunities are credible and actionable
- where opportunities are attractive but premature
- where opportunities should likely be rejected

### 5. Governance, Risk, And Constraint Findings
Capture findings on:
- policy and governance clarity
- decision rights
- security constraints
- privacy and compliance concerns
- model and data risk
- approval requirements
- traceability expectations
- failure or incident handling expectations

Also record:
- which issues would block broader scaling
- which issues can be tolerated in a focused next step
- where leadership assumptions appear weaker than reality

### 6. Data, Technology, And Platform Findings
Capture:
- data accessibility
- data quality
- platform fit
- tooling fragmentation
- integration complexity
- vendor dependency
- access bottlenecks
- shadow tooling or uncontrolled experimentation

Also note:
- whether the current environment supports disciplined progress
- whether technical fragmentation would weaken execution

### 7. Talent, Adoption, And Operating-Model Findings
Capture:
- who is currently driving AI activity
- whether capability is concentrated or thinly spread
- whether delivery ownership is clear
- whether business ownership is clear
- whether change readiness is strong or weak
- whether support expectations are realistic

### 8. Value And Feasibility Findings
Capture:
- evidence of near-term value opportunity
- evidence of broader strategic value
- evidence that value is overstated
- sequencing implications
- capacity constraints
- signs that opportunity appetite exceeds execution reality

### 9. Recommendation Development
Record:
- recommended next action
- rationale
- strongest supporting evidence
- strongest counterpoints
- key risks
- conditions to proceed
- likely owner
- required actions in the next 90 days

Recommendation categories:
- `Launch Focused Pilot`
- `Run Opportunity Prioritization Sprint`
- `Strengthen Foundations First`
- `Pause And Reframe`

### 10. Cross-Assessment Synthesis
Capture:
- strongest readiness signals
- weakest readiness signals
- strongest opportunity areas
- least credible opportunity areas
- common risk themes
- common ownership issues
- dependencies that should shape the 90-day path
- themes that should appear in the executive summary

### 11. Final Detailed Assessment Check
Before finalizing the output, confirm:
- the readiness picture is supported
- the opportunity shortlist is prioritized and defensible
- major constraints are visible
- the distinction between blockers and conditions is clear
- the next-step path is realistic and owned
- weak opportunities are visibly deprioritized

## Recommended Capture Format
Use these checklists alongside:
- interview notes
- readiness heatmaps
- opportunity prioritization sheets
- risk views
- recommendation sheets

The checklist is not the final deliverable. It is the working structure used to ensure the final deliverable is well supported.
