# Enterprise Agentic AI Readiness Assessment Finding Capture Checklists

## Purpose
This working document supports consistent capture of findings during delivery while assessing workflows, controls, readiness, and next-step recommendations.

Two finding-capture tracks are provided:

- `Quickfire Assessment`: for the 2-week decision-focused review
- `Detailed Assessment`: for the deeper 4 to 6 week review

## How To Use This Document
- use the relevant checklist during interviews, evidence review, and synthesis
- capture facts first and interpretation second
- note missing evidence explicitly
- separate current-state observations from recommendations
- identify where the evidence is strong, partial, or assumed

## Section A. Quickfire Assessment Finding Capture Checklist

### 1. Sponsor Intent And Decision Framing
- Confirm the sponsor objective in plain language.
- Capture the exact decision the sponsor wants to make.
- Note what must be decided now versus later.
- Capture any explicit scope limits.
- Note any non-negotiable constraints.
- Record what a useful final recommendation would need to answer.

### 2. Workflow In-Scope Validation
- Confirm the workflows actually being assessed.
- Record the business purpose of each workflow.
- Record the business owner of each workflow.
- Record the operational owner of each workflow.
- Note why each workflow was shortlisted.
- Capture whether any workflow appears out of scope or poorly defined.

### 3. Current-State Workflow Findings
For each workflow, capture:
- trigger point
- major steps
- tools and systems involved
- human roles involved
- outputs produced
- exception points
- approval points
- known workarounds
- pain points mentioned repeatedly

Also capture:
- where the workflow is clear and stable
- where the workflow is undocumented or inconsistent

### 4. Suitability Findings
For each workflow, capture findings on:
- multi-step workflow fit
- tool-use dependency
- exception complexity
- need for human judgment
- data and context availability
- measurability of outcomes
- reversibility of failure

Record:
- evidence supporting suitability
- evidence against suitability
- whether conventional automation may be a better fit
- provisional fit label: `High Fit`, `Conditional Fit`, `Low Fit`, `Not Suitable`

### 5. Readiness And Controls Findings
Capture findings on:
- governance ownership
- human oversight design
- approval requirements
- observability and evaluation readiness
- traceability and accountability
- security and access boundaries
- failure containment
- operational support ownership

Record:
- controls that already exist
- controls that are missing
- controls that are informal only
- issues that could block a pilot immediately
- overall readiness label: `Ready Now`, `Ready With Conditions`, `Not Ready Yet`, `Do Not Proceed`

### 6. Evidence Quality
For each workflow, note:
- what evidence was reviewed
- whether evidence was direct or second-hand
- whether examples were representative
- whether metrics were available
- where assumptions had to be made
- where follow-up evidence is still needed

### 7. Value And Risk Signals
Capture:
- signs of business value
- signs of operational efficiency gain
- signs of quality improvement potential
- signs of customer or service impact
- signs of material risk
- signs of workflow instability
- signs of ownership ambiguity

### 8. Recommendation Formation
For each workflow, record:
- preliminary recommendation
- key reasons supporting that recommendation
- conditions that would need to be met to move forward
- reasons to defer or reject
- most important risks if the workflow proceeds
- likely next owner for action
- required actions to close major gaps
- target timing for major gap closure actions

### 9. Cross-Workflow Synthesis
Capture:
- which workflows are strongest candidates
- which workflows are weak candidates
- common control gaps across workflows
- recurring dependency issues
- recurring stakeholder concerns
- themes that should appear in the executive summary
- dependencies that should appear in the 90-day plan
- decision gates that should appear in the 90-day plan

### 10. Final Quickfire Readiness Check
Before finalizing the output, confirm:
- each workflow has a clear recommendation category
- each recommendation is evidence-based
- missing evidence is visible
- key risks are explicit
- conditions to proceed are practical
- the next move is clear enough for executive discussion
- gap owners and timing are explicit
- the immediate 30-day action is visible

## Section B. Detailed Assessment Finding Capture Checklist

### 1. Assessment Intent And Operating Context
- Capture the sponsor objective and broader assessment intent.
- Record the decisions this assessment is expected to support.
- Capture any strategic context relevant to workflow prioritization.
- Note broader governance or operating-model concerns raised early.
- Record major dependencies on other transformation activity.

### 2. Workflow Portfolio Findings
For each workflow in scope, capture:
- business context
- process context
- operating context
- strategic relevance
- relationship to other workflows
- priority level

Also record:
- whether the workflow should stay in scope
- whether the workflow definition needs refinement

### 3. Detailed Current-State Findings
Capture:
- intake conditions
- step-by-step execution flow
- handoffs across teams
- systems and tools used
- decision points
- approval and review steps
- exception handling paths
- escalation paths
- rework loops
- current outputs

Also note:
- where execution differs from documented process
- where execution depends on tribal knowledge
- where the workflow is fragmented across systems or teams

### 4. Stakeholder Findings
Capture for each key stakeholder:
- role in the workflow
- perspective on workflow value
- perspective on workflow pain points
- perspective on automation or AI suitability
- view of current controls
- concerns about failure, oversight, or change adoption

Also note:
- where stakeholders are aligned
- where stakeholders disagree
- where ownership is unclear

### 5. Detailed Suitability Findings
For each workflow, assess and capture findings on:
- workflow structure
- level of multi-step coordination required
- dependence on tools and external systems
- dependence on fragmented context
- exception and edge-case frequency
- judgment complexity
- tolerance for wrong answers or failed actions
- outcome measurability

Record:
- evidence for strong fit
- evidence for limited or conditional fit
- reasons the workflow may be a poor candidate
- whether a narrower pilot scope would make sense

### 6. Detailed Readiness Findings
Capture findings on:
- governance ownership and decision rights
- approval models
- human-in-the-loop expectations
- monitoring and observability readiness
- traceability and auditability
- operational support model
- security constraints
- access and permission boundaries
- evaluation readiness
- change readiness

Also record:
- which gaps are fundamental blockers
- which gaps are manageable conditions
- which gaps can be deferred until pilot design
- overall readiness label: `Ready Now`, `Ready With Conditions`, `Not Ready Yet`, `Do Not Proceed`

### 7. Data And Systems Findings
Capture:
- quality of data available to support workflow execution
- stability of source systems
- integration complexity
- manual workarounds
- access bottlenecks
- dependence on unstructured information
- reporting limitations

Also note:
- whether the workflow has enough accessible context to support reliable execution
- whether missing or poor-quality data would undermine a pilot

### 8. Risk And Failure Findings
Capture:
- where failure could create customer harm
- where failure could create compliance or audit issues
- where failure could create operational disruption
- where failure is reversible
- where failure requires human containment
- where no clear containment path exists

Also record:
- likely failure modes
- current controls that reduce risk
- controls that would be needed before pilot

### 9. Value And Feasibility Findings
Capture:
- evidence of value opportunity
- evidence of cost or effort reduction
- evidence of service, speed, or quality improvement
- signs that value is overstated or unclear
- practical feasibility concerns
- likely sequencing considerations

### 10. Pilot-Readiness Findings
Capture:
- whether a pilot owner is apparent
- whether the workflow can be narrowed sensibly
- whether support ownership is clear
- whether review cadence can be established
- whether success measures are definable
- whether control conditions can be put in place in a reasonable timeframe

### 11. Recommendation Development
For each workflow, record:
- recommendation category
- rationale
- strongest supporting evidence
- strongest counterpoints
- key risks
- conditions to proceed
- likely next action
- likely owner
- required actions to close key gaps
- target timing for gap closure actions

Recommendation categories:
- `Proceed To Pilot`
- `Proceed With Narrowed Scope`
- `Defer Until Gaps Are Closed`
- `Do Not Pursue`

### 12. Cross-Workflow And Portfolio Findings
Capture:
- strongest workflows across the portfolio
- weakest workflows across the portfolio
- common control gaps
- common data issues
- common ownership issues
- common change-readiness themes
- sequencing implications across workflows
- themes that should shape the final executive narrative
- dependencies that should shape the 90-day path
- decision gates that should shape the 90-day path

### 13. Final Detailed Assessment Check
Before finalizing the output, confirm:
- each workflow has a supported recommendation
- the distinction between blockers and conditions is clear
- cross-workflow themes are visible
- missing evidence is acknowledged
- pilot-readiness implications are explicit
- the next-step path is realistic and owned
- gap owners and timing are explicit
- dependencies and decision gates are visible

## Recommended Capture Format
Use these checklists alongside:
- interview notes
- workflow scorecards
- readiness heatmaps
- gap sheets
- recommendation sheets

The checklist is not the final deliverable. It is the working structure used to ensure the final deliverable is well supported.
