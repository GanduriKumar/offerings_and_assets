Enterprise Agentic AI Readiness Assessment Delivery Guide

This guide explains how the Enterprise Agentic AI Readiness Assessment is delivered in two versions: a 2-week Quickfire Assessment for immediate pilot decisions, and a 4 to 6 week Detailed Assessment for deeper workflow, control, and pilot-readiness review.

In both cases, the goal is the same: provide leadership with a clear view of where agentic AI is credible, what is ready to move forward, what conditions need to be addressed, and what should happen next.

What The Assessment Is Designed To Do

The assessment is intended to help an organization answer four practical questions:

  1. Which workflows are genuinely suitable for agentic AI?
  2. What is ready for pilot now, not just in principle?
  3. Which readiness, governance, and control conditions matter most?
  4. Should the organization proceed, narrow scope, defer, or stop?

Assessment Variants

Quickfire Assessment

Best used when leadership needs a bounded decision quickly.

  • duration: 2 weeks
  • scope: 2 to 4 workflows
  • focus: fit, readiness, key control gaps, and immediate next move
  • output emphasis: executive decision support

Detailed Assessment

Best used when the organization needs a more developed view before launching a larger pilot or shaping a broader operating approach.

  • duration: 4 to 6 weeks
  • scope: 3 to 6 workflows, depending on complexity
  • focus: deeper workflow analysis, stakeholder alignment, control design implications, and pilot planning
  • output emphasis: decision support plus structured pilot-readiness path

Core Process Stages

The assessment follows the same broad process in both variants. The difference is the depth of evidence, number of stakeholders involved, and level of detail in the output.

Stage 1. Scope And Alignment

Objective: establish the assessment scope, align on the sponsor objective, and confirm the workflows to be reviewed.

Typical activities:

  • confirm the executive objective and decision context
  • identify the workflows in scope
  • align on key stakeholders and evidence sources
  • confirm the expected timeline and final readout date

Typical outputs:

  • scope note
  • workflow list
  • stakeholder map
  • evidence request list

Stage 2. Evidence Collection

Objective: gather the information needed to understand how each workflow operates today and what constraints shape it.

Typical activities:

  • review workflow steps, handoffs, and business objectives
  • collect current-state materials such as SOPs, process maps, and examples
  • understand systems, tools, approvals, and exception paths
  • capture available performance indicators and control considerations

Typical outputs:

  • current-state workflow summary
  • evidence log
  • stakeholder notes

Stage 3. Workflow Suitability Review

Objective: determine whether agentic AI is a strong fit for each workflow.

Typical review dimensions:

  • multi-step workflow fit
  • system and tool dependency
  • exception complexity
  • human oversight needs
  • availability of data and context
  • measurability of outcomes

Typical outputs:

  • workflow suitability scorecard
  • fit summary by workflow

Stage 4. Readiness And Controls Review

Objective: assess whether the organization has the operating conditions required to pilot safely and manage execution with confidence.

Typical review dimensions:

  • governance ownership
  • approval and oversight points
  • observability and evaluation readiness
  • traceability and accountability
  • security boundaries
  • failure containment
  • support ownership

Typical outputs:

  • readiness heatmap
  • governance and control gap view

Stage 5. Recommendation Development

Objective: translate the findings into clear decisions and practical next steps.

Recommendation categories:

  • Proceed To Pilot
  • Proceed With Narrowed Scope
  • Defer Until Gaps Are Closed
  • Do Not Pursue

Typical outputs:

  • recommendation sheet by workflow
  • conditions to proceed
  • next-step path

Stage 6. Executive Readout

Objective: present the assessment in a concise, decision-ready format.

Typical readout structure:

  1. assessment objective and scope
  2. workflows reviewed
  3. overall view across the workflow set
  4. workflow-by-workflow recommendation
  5. key readiness and control considerations
  6. recommended next-step path

Typical outputs:

  • executive decision summary
  • readout deck

Quickfire Assessment Delivery

When To Use It

Use the Quickfire Assessment when the organization needs a fast, structured decision on a small number of workflows.

Typical Shape

  • senior-led assessment
  • focused interview set
  • limited but sufficient evidence review
  • rapid synthesis and recommendation development

Typical Timeline

Week 1

  • kickoff and scope confirmation
  • stakeholder interviews
  • evidence review
  • early workflow fit assessment

Week 2

  • readiness and controls review
  • recommendation development
  • executive readout preparation
  • final readout and discussion

Quickfire Deliverables

  • executive decision summary
  • workflow suitability scorecard
  • readiness heatmap
  • governance and control gap view
  • go / no-go recommendation set
  • 90-day next-step path

What Makes The Quickfire Useful

  • keeps the assessment tightly focused
  • helps leadership decide quickly
  • surfaces critical risks without over-expanding the scope
  • supports an immediate next-step decision

Detailed Assessment Delivery

When To Use It

Use the Detailed Assessment when the organization needs a deeper view before shaping a larger pilot, broader workflow portfolio, or more developed governance response.

Typical Shape

  • wider stakeholder participation
  • deeper current-state review
  • more detailed treatment of controls, ownership, and operational conditions
  • stronger emphasis on dependencies, sequencing, and pilot readiness

Typical Timeline

Week 1

  • kickoff and sponsor alignment
  • final scope definition
  • workflow selection and stakeholder mapping
  • evidence request launch

Week 2

  • business, delivery, and technology interviews
  • current-state workflow review
  • early suitability hypotheses

Week 3

  • detailed suitability review
  • readiness and control analysis
  • deeper assessment of dependencies and exception handling

Week 4

  • recommendation development
  • cross-workflow comparison
  • pilot-readiness implications

Weeks 5 To 6, If Required

  • additional stakeholder alignment
  • refinement of recommendation set
  • more detailed next-step planning
  • final executive readout

Detailed Deliverables

  • executive decision summary
  • workflow suitability scorecards
  • readiness heatmap across workflows
  • governance and control gap register
  • recommendation sheet by workflow
  • 90-day next-step path
  • pilot-readiness considerations
  • optional workshop or executive discussion pack

What Makes The Detailed Assessment Useful

  • provides a stronger basis for pilot planning
  • gives more depth on governance and control implications
  • helps align multiple stakeholders before action
  • supports more complex workflow decisions

Deliverable Descriptions

Executive Decision Summary

A concise summary of the decision context, the workflows assessed, the recommendations, and the immediate next move.

Workflow Suitability Scorecard

A structured assessment of how well each workflow fits agentic AI.

Readiness Heatmap

A clear view of where operating conditions are already in place and where they need to be strengthened.

Governance And Control Gap View

A focused summary of the most important conditions, ownership gaps, and control issues to address.

Recommendation Sheet

A clear recommendation for each workflow, with rationale, risks, conditions, and next step.

90-Day Next-Step Path

A structured plan showing what should happen next if the organization chooses to move forward.

Client Participation

The quality of the assessment depends on timely access to the right people and materials.

  • executive sponsor alignment
  • workflow owner interviews
  • delivery or operations stakeholder input
  • technology or platform stakeholder input where relevant
  • access to current-state workflow materials

How The Two Variants Differ

Dimension Quickfire Assessment Detailed Assessment
Duration 2 weeks 4 to 6 weeks
Typical workflow count 2 to 4 3 to 6
Stakeholder depth focused broader
Evidence depth targeted deeper
Output emphasis immediate decision support decision support plus pilot shaping
Best use case fast go / no-go view deeper pilot-readiness view

Closing Note

Both assessment variants are designed to produce practical guidance, not abstract commentary. The value of the process lies in its ability to connect workflow reality, governance discipline, and decision clarity in a form that leadership can use immediately.
