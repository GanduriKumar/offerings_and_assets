# [Brand Placeholder] Executive AI Readiness and Opportunity Assessment

## The Offer
**A fixed-fee, leadership-focused assessment that gives executive teams a credible outside view of enterprise AI readiness, the best near-term opportunity areas, the main risks to address, and the next move worth funding.**

This is the anchor product for organizations that want clarity before expanding pilots, committing larger budgets, or forcing a program structure before the basics are understood.

## Who This Is For
- CIOs, CTOs, CEOs, COOs, and business unit leaders deciding how seriously to invest in AI
- GCC leaders, transformation heads, and digital leaders shaping enterprise priorities
- executive sponsors who need a grounded view before authorizing broader rollout
- leadership teams that have experiments underway but no coherent decision picture

## The Buyer Problem
Most organizations do not lack AI ideas. They lack a credible way to separate:

- real readiness from aspirational positioning
- promising use cases from generic hype
- manageable risk from hidden exposure
- next-step priorities from unfocused experimentation

The executive questions are usually straightforward:

- Is the organization actually ready to scale AI beyond isolated trials?
- Where are the best practical opportunity areas right now?
- What risks or capability gaps would slow us down or create avoidable mistakes?
- What should leadership do next: proceed, narrow focus, strengthen foundations, or wait?

## The Promise
**In two to four weeks, leadership gets a practical outside-in view of AI readiness, opportunity areas, risk signals, and a recommended next action that can be defended with confidence.**

## What The Client Receives

### 1. Executive AI Readiness Heatmap
A concise view of current capability strength across areas such as:

- strategy and executive sponsorship
- business prioritization
- data and platform readiness
- governance and risk controls
- operating model and ownership
- talent, adoption, and change readiness

### 2. Opportunity Shortlist
A prioritized view of where AI can create practical near-term value, including:

- highest-potential opportunity areas
- why those areas matter now
- what conditions affect feasibility
- which ideas should be deprioritized

### 3. Risk And Constraint View
A focused view of the issues leadership should not ignore:

- governance gaps
- data or platform limitations
- security, privacy, or compliance concerns
- ownership ambiguity
- change and execution risks

### 4. Recommended Next Action
A direct recommendation on the most sensible next move, such as:

- run a focused pilot
- prioritize a use-case sprint
- strengthen foundations before scaling
- align governance and decision rights
- stop spreading effort across low-value experiments

### 5. 90-Day Action Path
A practical path for the next quarter covering:

- immediate leadership decisions
- capability or governance actions
- opportunity validation work
- pilot or roadmap shaping steps
